Acknowledgement of Country

Acknowledgements of Country are a respectful way to recognise the Traditional Custodians of the land on which this event will take place. They show awareness of history and support reconciliation by acknowledging the enduring connection of First Nations peoples to Country.

A Acknowledgement of Country can be made by anyone. A Welcome to Country is different: it is a cultural welcome that should be performed by a recognised Traditional Custodian.

Good practice for a meaningful acknowledgement

  • Use the correct Traditional Custodians for the event location.
  • Avoid generic wording that feels tokenistic; recognise ongoing connection to Country.
  • Do not claim to speak for Traditional Owners.
  • Check reliable sources before publishing your event.

Helpful resources

  • AIATSIS guidance on recognising Country
  • Reconciliation Australia
  • NIAA Acknowledgement of Country
